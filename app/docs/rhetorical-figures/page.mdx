# Rhetorical Figures

> "A figure of speech is a departure from the ordinary form of expression." — Henry Peacham

## Overview

Rhetorical figures (figures of speech) are linguistic devices that create special effects or emphasize ideas through unconventional word arrangements, repetitions, or meanings.

## Major Figures of Speech

### Antithesis (المقابلة)
Juxtaposing contrasting ideas in parallel structure.

**Examples:**
- "It was the best of times, it was the worst of times." — Dickens
- "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind." — Armstrong
- "To err is human; to forgive, divine." — Pope

### Chiasmus (التشاكس)
Reversing the structure of parallel phrases.

**Examples:**
- "Ask not what your country can do for you—ask what you can do for your country." — Kennedy
- "When the going gets tough, the tough get going."

### Anaphora (الاسترجاع)
Repeating words or phrases at the beginning of successive clauses.

**Examples:**
- "I have a dream... I have a dream... I have a dream..." — MLK
- "We shall fight on the beaches, we shall fight on the landing grounds..." — Churchill

### Epistrophe (الالتفات)
Repeating words or phrases at the end of successive clauses.

**Examples:**
- "...and that government of the people, by the people, for the people, shall not perish from the earth." — Lincoln

### Polysyndeton (الإطناب)
Using multiple conjunctions between words.

**Examples:**
- "I came and saw and conquered." — Caesar

### Asyndeton (الحذف)
Omitting conjunctions between words.

**Examples:**
- "I came, I saw, I conquered." — Caesar
- "Government of the people, by the people, for the people." — Lincoln

### Zeugma (الالتفاف)
Using one word to govern multiple other words in different ways.

**Example:** "She broke his heart and his promise."

### Metaphor (استعارة)
Direct comparison without "like" or "as."

### Simile (تشبيه)
Comparison using "like" or "as."

### Hyperbole (المبالغة)
Exaggeration for emphasis.

## Gorgianic Figures

Named after the Greek sophist Gorgias, these are balanced clauses with similar sounds:

- **Antithesis** - Opposing ideas
- **Parison** - Equal clauses
- **Isocolon** - Equal cola (phrases/clauses)
- **Homoioteleuton** - Similar endings
