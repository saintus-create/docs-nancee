# Shannon and Weaver Model

> "The fundamental problem of communication is that of reproducing at one point either exactly or approximately a message selected at another point." — Shannon & Weaver

## Overview

Claude Shannon and Warren Weaver's *The Mathematical Theory of Communication* (1949) established the foundational model of communication that continues to influence communication studies.

## The Model

```
Source → Encoder → Message → Channel → Decoder → Receiver
                  ↓
                Noise
```

## Components

### 1. Information Source
The origin of the message—what needs to be communicated.

### 2. Transmitter (Encoder)
Converts the message into signals suitable for transmission.

**Examples:**
- Telephone converts voice to electrical signals
- Pen converts thoughts to written words

### 3. Signal
The physical form of the message as it travels through the channel.

### 4. Channel
The medium through which signals travel.

**Examples:**
- Air (face-to-face)
- Wire (telephone)
- Radio waves (broadcast)

### 5. Receiver (Decoder)
Converts signals back into the original message.

### 6. Destination
The intended recipient of the message.

## The Problem of Noise

### Types of Noise
1. **Physical noise** - External sounds, static
2. **Semantic noise** - Different interpretations of meaning
3. **Psychological noise** - Prejudices, biases

### Impact of Noise
- Reduces fidelity of transmission
- Requires redundancy in messages
- Necessitates feedback mechanisms

## Three Levels of Problems

Weaver identified three levels of communication problems:

1. **Technical problems** - Accuracy of message transmission
2. **Semantic problems** - Precision of meaning
3. **Effectiveness problems** - How successfully does the message affect behavior?

## Limitations

1. **Linear model** - Ignores feedback
2. **Information vs. meaning** - Doesn't capture semantic complexity
3. **Human context** - Oversimplifies human communication

## Legacy

Despite limitations, the model:
- Established communication as a measurable science
- Influenced all subsequent communication theory
- Provided vocabulary for communication analysis
