# Marshall McLuhan

> "The medium is the message." — Marshall McLuhan

## Overview

Marshall McLuhan (1911-1980) was a pioneering media theorist whose work on the impact of communication technologies continues to influence how we understand media today.

## Key Concepts

### 1. The Medium is the Message
The medium through which a message is communicated is more important than the content itself because it shapes how we think and organize society.

### 2. Global Village
Electronic media creates a "global village" where information travels instantly and people are connected across distances.

### 3. Hot and Cold Media
- **Hot media** - High definition, engages one sense intensely (film, print)
- **Low media** - Low definition, requires participation (telephone, TV)

### 4. Sensory Ratios
Each medium changes our sensory ratios—how we balance sight, sound, touch, and other senses.

## Major Works

### Understanding Media: The Extensions of Man (1964)
McLuhan's most influential work, examining how media technologies affect human perception and society.

### The Gutenberg Galaxy (1962)
Analyzes how print technology transformed Western consciousness.

## Famous Quotations

1. "The medium is the message."
2. "The medium is the massage." (play on words)
3. "We shape our tools and thereafter our tools shape us."
4. "There are no passengers on Spaceship Earth."

## McLuhan's Laws of Media

1. **Extension** - Every medium extends some human faculty
2. **Obsolescence** - Every medium makes some previous medium obsolete
3. **Retrieval** - When pushed to extremes, a medium reverses into its opposite
4. **Reversal** - The content of a medium is always another medium

## Influence and Criticism

### Influenced
- Media studies
- Technology criticism
- Cultural studies
- Design theory

### Criticized
- Lack of empirical evidence
- Oversimplification
- Deterministic approach
