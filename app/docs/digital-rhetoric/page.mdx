# Digital Rhetoric

> "Computer-mediated communication creates new possibilities for rhetorical action." — Sharon Jarvis

## Overview

Digital rhetoric examines how rhetorical theory and practice apply to computer-mediated communication, including social media, websites, and online discourse.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Networked Rhetoric
Rhetoric conducted through digital networks, characterized by:
- Hypertextual connections
- Multimodal compositions
- Distributed authorship

### 2. Remix Culture
Borrowing, mixing, and transforming existing texts:
- Memes
- Video essays
- Mashups

### 3. Participatory Culture
Users as active participants, not passive consumers:
- User-generated content
- Crowdsourcing
- Social media activism

### 4. Multimodality
Text combined with:
- Images
- Video
- Audio
- Interactivity

## Digital Rhetorical Situations

### affordances
Unique capabilities of digital media:
- Immediacy
- Interactivity
- Accessibility
- Persistence

### Constraints
Limitations of digital discourse:
- Digital divide
- Information overload
- Privacy concerns

## Applications

### Social Media Rhetoric
- Twitter/X debates
- Facebook discourse
- Instagram visual rhetoric
- TikTok video essays

### Online Persuasion
- Digital advertising
- Viral marketing
- Online activism

### Technical Communication
- Help documentation
- Online tutorials
- Knowledge bases

## Ethical Considerations

1. **Privacy** - Data collection and surveillance
2. **Accessibility** - Digital divide and inclusivity
3. **Misinformation** - Fake news and deepfakes
4. **Identity** - Anonymity and persona
