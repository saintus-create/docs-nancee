# Argumentation Theory

> "Rhetoric is the study of misunderstanding and its cures." — I.A. Richards

## Overview

Argumentation theory is the interdisciplinary study of how humans argue, reason, and persuade through discourse. It draws upon logic, rhetoric, philosophy, psychology, and communication studies.

## Key Theories

### The New Rhetoric (البلاغة الجديدة)
Chaim Perelman and Lucie Olbrechts-Tyteca's foundational work that extended rhetorical theory beyond classical limits to encompass all forms of argumentation.

### Informal Logic (المنطق غير الصوري)
The study of reasoning and argument structure outside formal logical systems, developed by scholars like Douglas Walton and Ralph Johnson.

### Pragma-dialectics (الحجاج البراغماتي)
The theory developed by Frans van Eemeren and Rob Grootendorst that analyzes argumentation as a form of strategic maneuvering within a dialectical process.

## Fundamental Concepts

### Argument (الحجة)
A set of propositions, one of which (the conclusion) is claimed to follow from the others (the premises).

### Claim (ادعاء)
The assertion or thesis that an arguer wants the audience to accept.

### Warrant (الضامن)
The underlying reasoning that connects evidence to a claim; the logical bridge between premises and conclusion.

### Evidence (الدليل)
Facts, data, or examples used to support premises in an argument.

### Backing (الدعم)
Additional support or evidence that backs up the warrant when it is challenged.

## Types of Arguments

1. **Deductive Arguments** - Conclusions necessarily follow from premises
2. **Inductive Arguments** - Conclusions probabilistically follow from premises
3. **Analogical Arguments** - Drawing conclusions based on similarities
4. **Causal Arguments** - Establishing cause-effect relationships
