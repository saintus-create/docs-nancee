# Communication Theory

> "Communication is the fundamental social process." — Wilbur Schramm

## Overview

Communication theory examines how humans create, transmit, and interpret messages across various contexts and media.

## Foundational Models

### Shannon-Weaver Model (نموذج شانون- ويفر)

The Mathematical Theory of Communication (1949) by Claude Shannon and Warren Weaver established the fundamental linear model of communication:

```
Source → Encoder → Message → Channel → Decoder → Receiver → Noise
```

**Key Concepts:**
- **Source** - The origin of the message
- **Encoder** - Converts message into transmittable signals
- **Channel** - The medium through which messages travel
- **Decoder** - Interprets received signals
- **Noise** - Any interference that distorts the message
- **Feedback** - Response from receiver back to source

### Schramm's Model

Wilbur Schramm refined the Shannon-Weaver model to include:
- Shared fields of experience
- Interaction between source and receiver
- The role of interpretation in communication

## Theoretical Traditions

### Process Tradition
Viewing communication as a linear or interactive process of message transmission.

### Rhetorical Tradition
Viewing communication as purposeful action aimed at persuasion and identification.

### Semiotic Tradition
Viewing communication as the production and interpretation of signs and symbols.

## Key Theories

### 1. Uses and Gratifications (الاستخدامات والإشباعات)
Audiences actively choose media to satisfy specific needs and desires.

### 2. Agenda Setting (تحديد议事日程)
Media influences what audiences think about, not what they think.

### 3. Framing (التأطير)
Media influences how audiences interpret events through selective presentation.

### 4. Cultivation Theory (نظرية الزراعة)
Television cultivates viewers' perceptions of social reality over time.

## Mass Communication

### Mass Media Effects
- **Hypodermic Needle Theory** - Direct, powerful effects
- **Limited Effects Theory** - Minimal, mediated effects
- **Cultural Effects Theory** - Long-term, indirect effects

### The Mass Audience Concept
The mass audience is characterized by:
- Large, anonymous, heterogeneous audiences
- One-way, asynchronous communication
- Professional message production
