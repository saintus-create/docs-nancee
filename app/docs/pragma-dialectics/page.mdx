# Pragma-dialectics

> "Argumentation is a complex communicative activity aimed at resolving a difference of opinion." — van Eemeren & Grootendorst

## Overview

Pragma-dialectics is a theory of argumentation developed by Frans van Eemeren and Rob Grootendorst that combines logical analysis with pragmatic communication theory. It provides a systematic approach to analyzing and evaluating argumentative discourse.

## Core Principles

### 1. Ideal Model of Critical Discussion

The pragma-dialectical theory proposes an ideal model of critical discussion with four stages:

1. **Confrontation** - Parties identify and clarify the difference of opinion
2. **Opening** - Parties establish rules and starting points
3. **Argumentation** - The protagonist advances arguments to defend their standpoint
4. **Concluding** - Parties evaluate the outcome of the argumentation

### 2. Ten Rules for Critical Discussion

1. Freedom Rule - Parties must not prevent each other from advancing standpoints
2. Obligation to Defend - A standpoint must be defended if asked
3. Freedom Rule (2) - Parties must not prevent each other from casting doubt
4. Starting Points - No unsupported premises may be introduced
5. Relevant Authority - A standpoint can only be defended by argument relevant to it
6. Validity - The defense must be logically valid or explicitly justified
7. Closure - A failed defense means the protagonist gives up the standpoint
8. Accommodation - Arguments must be accepted or the procedure must be questioned
9. Invalid Defense - A failed defense means the antagonist gives up doubt
10. Language Use - Parties must express themselves clearly and avoid equivocation

### 3. Strategic Maneuvering

Pragma-dialectics acknowledges that real argumentative practice involves "strategic maneuvering"—the simultaneous pursuit of rhetorical effectiveness and dialectical reasonableness.

## Key Concepts

### Difference of Opinion (اختلاف الرأي)
The starting point of argumentation, where parties hold conflicting views on a particular issue.

### Standpoint (الموقف)
The position a party takes with respect to a particular statement or issue.

### Argumentation Scheme (مخطط الحجاج)
A typical pattern of reasoning used in argumentation, such as:
- Argument from example
- Argument from analogy
- Causal argument
- Argument from authority

### Critical Question (السؤال النقدي)
A question that tests the soundness of an argumentation scheme.

## Applications

- **Legal argumentation** - Analyzing courtroom discourse
- **Political debate** - Examining political speeches and discussions
- **Academic discourse** - Evaluating scholarly argumentation
- **Mediation** - Resolving conflicts through structured dialogue
