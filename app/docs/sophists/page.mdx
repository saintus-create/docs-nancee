# The Sophists

> "The Sophists were the first to systematize rhetoric as a teachable art." — George Kennedy

## Overview

The Sophists were itinerant teachers in ancient Greece (5th century BC) who taught rhetoric, virtue, and practical wisdom for public life. Despite Plato's criticism, they significantly influenced Greek thought and education.

## Major Sophists

### 1. Protagoras (c. 490-420 BC)
- "Man is the measure of all things"
- Taught rhetoric and virtue
- Developed grammatical studies
- Famous for "The Two Logoi" (two sides of any question)

### 2. Gorgias (c. 485-380 BC)
- Master of epideictic oratory
- *Encomium of Helen* - defense of Helen of Troy
- *Palamedes* - defense of ODysseus
- Known for elaborate, ornamental style

### 3. Prodicus (c. 465-395 BC)
- Studied semantics and language
- Developed concept of proper names
- Taught moral education through mythology

### 4. Hippias (c. 485-415 BC)
- Multi-disciplinary teacher
- Known for mathematical and astronomical knowledge
- Claimed to memorize 50 names at once

### 5. Antiphon (c. 480-411 BC)
- Sophist and logographer
- Developed theory of rhetoric
- Wrote tetralogies (hypothetical speeches)

### 6. Isocrates (436-338 BC)
- Student of Socrates
- Founded major rhetorical school
- Educational treatises
- Panhellenic political vision

## Plato's Critique

Plato criticized Sophists in several dialogues:

### 1. *Gorgias*
- Rhetoric as flattery (kolakeia)
- Not a true art (techne)
- Concerned with persuasion, not truth

### 2. *Phaedrus*
- Rhetoric needs philosophical foundation
- Love (eros) as rhetorical inspiration
- Division of souls and speeches

### 3. *Sophist*
- Sophists as image-makers
- Imitation without knowledge

## Defense of the Sophists

Modern scholars recognize Sophists' contributions:

1. **Educational innovation** - First systematic education
2. **Democratization** - Taught anyone who could pay
3. **Linguistic studies** - Grammar, semantics, rhetoric
4. **Pragmatic wisdom** - Practical ethics for public life

## Legacy

- Foundation of rhetorical education
- Methods of argument
- Concepts of relativity and perspectivism
- Political and legal training
